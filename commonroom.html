The Common Room

After the sorting ceremony and a wonderful banquet it’s time to go to your common room. Your Prefect introduces himself to your house as August Rabbot. You follow your Prefect through the castle. The castle is even bigger than you imagined it to be, you think to yourself as you keep on walking. You pass a group of giggling girls, followed by two boys holding a note on a piece of parchment written in red ink, which looks to be a love letter. You would be interested to know what the love letter said if your eyes weren’t so heavy and your stomach was less full from the amazing food. When you arrive at the end of a long corridor you can see a simple, wooden oak door. August tells you this is the entrance to your common room and he explains how to open the door: “To enter your common room you need to go through this door, but it’s locked with a puzzle! Solve this and you may enter".

You look and see that there’s something written on the door: “I alternate, but do not create, I will equate but cannot deteriorate."
